Axolotl

Priced deliberately in the middle of the pack, not the cheapest or the most valuable pick — the Axolotl was never meant to be a better fish, just a different kind of resident. It still grows up and gets hungry like everything else, and it's still born with one of the six personalities below, but three small things set it apart:

  • It has real favorite foods — Brine Shrimp, Bloodworms, and Worms (see Mechanics for Treats) — shown right in its Inspector. Feeding it one of these earns a delighted reaction, never a bigger stat than any other treat gives.
  • It rests on the tank floor far more often, and for much longer, than any fish — with its own closed-eyes look while it does.
  • It never schools, even with another Axolotl — solitary by nature, unlike the fish species above.

It still sleeps in containers exactly like a fish would, and can turn up as a random starter, a stress-test spawn, or an Emergency Aquarium Welfare grant, just like any other peaceful species.

Personality

Every fish, regardless of species, is born with exactly one of six personalities, which shapes its steering, feeding urgency, and where it chooses to sleep:

  • Friendly — this game's "caring" personality: follows the mouse, and drifts toward the group's average position rather than wandering off alone.
  • Explorer — patrols constantly, changing direction far more often than the others in a steady, wide-ranging sweep.
  • Shy — flees once the mouse gets close, hiding behind the nearest decoration if one's available.
  • Greedy — chases food noticeably faster (1.6× speed) than everyone else.
  • Lazy — a reduced base speed from the moment it's born, and changes direction far less often.
  • Playful — changes direction often and varies its speed wildly, in bursts of energy rather than Explorer's steady patrol; also gets an extra relationship boost out of successfully waking a friend up.

Sleepy is separate from all six above — an independent trait rolled once at birth alongside the personality, so a fish can be, say, Greedy and Sleepy at once. A normal wake-up attempt practically never works on a Sleepy fish; see Mechanics for how wake-ups actually resolve.
