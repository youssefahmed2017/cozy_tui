Plant and Driftwood are purely visual — fish steer around them, nothing more. Selling any decoration back refunds half its price.

Who gets a home

Every night, each fish looks for a container (a decoration with capacity > 0) to sleep in, in this priority order:

  1. Its own favorite spot, if there's still room.
  2. A bonded tankmate's container, if there's room there.
  3. Whichever container with room is nearest.
  4. If nothing has room, it sleeps on the open tank floor instead.

Once inside, a fish is genuinely removed from view until it wakes — click the decoration to see who's currently home. An Explorer occasionally shuffles to a different container than its usual pick, echoing its constant-patrol restlessness while awake.

Enter the room

A container's Inspector has an Enter button into a quieter, dedicated interior view — beds and pillows, who's asleep versus lingering awake, and a live *boop* if a Sleepy fish is being woken (see Mechanics). It keeps updating while it's open rather than freezing at the moment you looked in, and the whole room leaves together once everyone inside is awake, instead of trickling out one at a time.
