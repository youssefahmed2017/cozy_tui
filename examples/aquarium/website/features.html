Under the hood

Every fish is running a small simulation.

Nothing here is scripted. What you see in the tank is the output of systems that keep running whether you're watching or not.

Temperament

Personality & Sleepy

Every fish is born Friendly, Explorer, Shy, Greedy, Lazy, or Playful — it decides how they swim, flee, and choose where to sleep. Sleepy is separate and stacks with any of the six: a Sleepy Greedy fish still races for food, but genuinely sleeps in past everyone else — a same-container Friend or Neutral tankmate has to actually wake it, resisted attempt after attempt, though never forever.

Habitat

Container homes

The rock formation sleeps two, the castle sleeps four. Fish queue by preference — favorite spot, then a bonded tankmate's spot, then whatever's nearest — and once inside, they're genuinely gone from view. Click the furniture to peek in, or Enter for a quieter, live view of who's asleep, who's stirring, and the room leaving together once everyone's up.

Bond

Earned relationships

Nothing is assigned at birth. Every pair of fish carries a private score that moves with what actually happens between them — waking one another, sleeping side by side, giving up a bed so someone else could have it — and fades if left alone. You never see the number, only what it adds up to: Rival, Dislikes, Neutral, Friend, Best Friend.

Cycle

Day & night

Temperature and light drift on one continuous curve, warmest at noon and coldest at midnight. Night is a hard stop — fish that aren't starving simply sleep. Mornings occasionally open on a small scene between two friends, one just barely still asleep.

Economy

Upkeep & visitors

Fish and decorations earn daily visitor income based on how well-kept the tank looks. Let it run completely dry and Emergency Aquarium Welfare quietly steps in — a small fresh start, not a game over.

Continuity

Save, load, pause

Name a save once and every later save writes back into the same file. The load screen can rename, duplicate, or delete freely, pausing actually freezes the simulation — not just the screen — and Ctrl+C returns to the Main Menu from anywhere, with a Resume button to pick your session back up exactly where it was.

Cloud

Cloud Saves

Set up a Cloud Key from Settings and every save syncs automatically — no account, no password. Reinstall Windows, move to a new PC, whatever: enter the same key and restore any of your saves back onto it. Losing the key means losing access, so it's shown once with a Copy button and a plain warning to write it down.

Feeding

Treats

Beyond bulk Fish Food — dropped in the water, eaten by whoever's nearest — the Shop also sells five named treats: Brine Shrimp, Worms, Bloodworms, Plankton, and Pizza. Each is fed directly to one chosen fish from its own Inspector, a deliberate gesture rather than a free-for-all. Pizza is a single-serving indulgence every fish loves for no particular reason — the rest are ordinary, until you meet the one resident with real favorites.

New resident

Axolotl

Not a fish with extra mechanics bolted on — a different kind of resident entirely. It rests on the tank floor far more than anything else (with its own closed-eyes look while doing it), never schools even with another Axolotl, and has real favorite foods: Brine Shrimp, Bloodworms, Worms. Feeding it one earns a delighted reaction, never a bigger stat — same price range, same growth, same everything else. The difference is personality, not power.

Milestones

Achievements

Eleven account-wide milestones — a first friendship, a first baby, setting up Cloud Saves — that survive a new save or a fresh aquarium, because they're not tied to either one. The Achievements menu never hides what's still locked: every name and description is visible from the start, so it doubles as a light to-do list of things worth trying.

Surprise

Random Events

Roughly one night in eight, something happens on its own: a stray fish wanders in and stays, free of charge; a storm rolls through and sends every awake fish diving for the nearest shelter until it passes; a few dollars turn up loose in the gravel; or a fish just does a little spin, for no reason at all. Deliberately rare — meant to be noticed, not routine.

At night

Dreams

Sleeping fish occasionally dream — shown as a 💭 you can click open for a small, looping animated scene with a plain-language caption underneath, in case the picture alone doesn't give it away. Personality leans which kind it gets (a Greedy fish dreams about food often, never exclusively), and real memory shapes it further: a recent shark scare raises the odds of dreaming about it again, a recent moment with a Friend nudges toward dreaming about them, and losing a tankmate can bring them back for as long as that memory survives. The rare nightmare is the one dream with real consequences: the fish wakes up scared, then quietly goes to sleep beside a Friend if it has one.

Diary

Fish Memory Log

Every fish keeps its own short diary, right in its Inspector — real, already-tracked things that happened to it, not invented flavor: a favorite treat, waking a friend up, becoming rivals with someone, surviving a storm, a tankmate that isn't around anymore. Capped at ten entries, oldest dropped first, so it stays a snapshot of what's still fresh rather than a scrolling wall.

Testing

Cheat Console

Press ` for a small typed-command console: spawn a fish for free, set money or food directly, or buy something from the Shop without grinding for it first. Every command is a real effect on the live tank through the exact same code the Shop and Inspector already use — there's no separate cheat-only path underneath.
